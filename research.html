Research Focus Areas

Because research is dynamic and continually evolving, project topics may change; students are encouraged to visit participating faculty websites to learn about their most current research directions.

IoT Sensor Security & Privacy

Develop secure communication protocols and privacy-preserving techniques for Internet of Things (IoT) sensor networks. Research focuses on lightweight cryptography, secure data aggregation, and protection against various cyber attacks targeting sensor infrastructures in smart cities and defense applications.

Lightweight cryptographic protocols
Privacy-preserving data aggregation
Attack detection and mitigation
IoT Security Privacy Cryptography

Smart Environmental Sensing

Design and deploy advanced sensor networks for environmental monitoring and climate research. Focus on developing energy-efficient sensors, data fusion techniques, and machine learning algorithms for real-time environmental data analysis and prediction systems.

Multi-modal sensor integration
Energy harvesting techniques
Real-time data analytics
Environmental Sensors Data Fusion Machine Learning

Trajectory Privacy Preservation in Mobile Sensor Networks

Develop privacy-preserving techniques for mobile sensor networks that protect user location and movement patterns. Research focuses on cryptographic protocols and data obfuscation methods that enable useful data collection while maintaining individual privacy.

Location privacy algorithms
Trajectory obfuscation techniques
Secure data aggregation
Privacy Mobile Sensors Cryptography

Mobile Video Authenticity Verification

Develop algorithms and systems to verify the authenticity of mobile video content and detect tampering. Research focuses on digital forensics techniques, blockchain-based verification, and real-time authentication systems for mobile sensor data.

Digital forensics algorithms
Blockchain verification systems
Real-time authenticity detection
Digital Forensics Video Security Authenticity

Quantum Technologies & Applications

Explore quantum sensing technologies, quantum communication protocols, and quantum-enhanced sensor systems. Research focuses on practical applications of quantum technologies for enhanced sensing capabilities and secure communications in defense and civilian contexts.

Quantum sensing and metrology
Quantum key distribution
Quantum-enhanced sensor networks
Quantum Sensing Quantum Communications Emerging Tech

Projects by REU Students

TeleBot Project

Three of our REU students significantly contributed to the TeleBot project, where the project received more than 25 media coverages from the US, Canada, UK, and the US. The prototype allows a disabled person to control the robot remotely, see everything the robot "sees," and interact with members of the public. The Telebot stands six feet tall, weighs about 75 pounds, and is controlled remotely. The dedication and passion of all of our REU undergraduate student members on the project and their countless hours of volunteer work for this project are priceless.
